The Vision Begins

"Back in 1991, I was living in New York City when God reached out to me, and I began to seek the Lord with my life. Soon after, the Lord spoke to me through a vision. I had just come from church and was trying to get some sleep when suddenly it seemed as though the Lord took me up into the sky. I could see the city far below me, and I could see snow falling around me in the vision. Then the scene below me in the vision changed instantly, and in my spirit, I felt as though I was in South Carolina. I could see all the trees below me, and God brought me back down to the earth in this vision, and I recognized the location where I landed.

It was my childhood home. In the vision, it was as though I was being led by someone, but I never could see them. I was led to a field, which I remembered from when I was young, and I was led across it. Though when I first saw it, I wondered how I could ever cross because the field appeared to be freshly plowed. As I wondered, a light appeared near me in the field, and it was as though a road appeared before me from one side of the field to the other side. The road was a glass road with pits of fire under the glass all the way across. Then I went down in the ditch, which I also remembered from when I was young. Once I crossed on the other side of the ditch and made it out to the home of my aunt, my grandmother's home was right next to it. I saw a pretty apple tree in the backyard. As I was walking toward the sight, something caught my attention, and I looked back and saw in the front yard a man sitting in a chair. He had his head down - I couldn't see his face because it was twilight dark. I went toward the sight asking who it was. As I got closer, everything vanished. Then the vision ended, and I awoke back home in New York City.

The first thought that struck me was that God had spoken to me to return to my childhood home in South Carolina and he would show me what it was that he had for me to do. Yet I didn't fully understand how I was to go about it at the time. In New York, I had a very good job for which I was thankful, and I needed a way to provide for my family, not to mention I had gone through some difficult experiences back in South Carolina. I wrestled with God over this decision for a number of months. Then one Sunday morning, my Pastor, Bishop Bonner, preached a powerful sermon, and it seemed to me as though he was speaking directly to me. In the message, he said that if you have a difficult decision to make in your service to the Lord, go on a ten-day fast and trust the Lord to give you the answer.

I took that for the gospel because I certainly had some decisions to make. So, I began fasting and praying, but as the tenth day drew near, I almost felt as though God wouldn't speak to me. Yet on the morning of the tenth day, I got my answer. The strange thing that I experienced that morning was that I could hear the sounds of birds chirping loud and clear, which you never hear in New York. I heard that sound in the subway train and in the hallways of the office building in which I worked, and in the elevator of the office building in which I worked.

As soon as I exited the elevator, I was standing right next to my boss's desk, and he was sitting right there. Without a second thought, I spoke up and told him that I was giving him two weeks' notice and I would be leaving the company. He asked if I had a job lined up or if I needed a recommendation, "NO SIR," I answered him. He didn't understand it all, and honestly, I didn't either, but I knew I just needed to trust the Lord.

Those next two weeks were hectic, finishing up my work by day and getting ready to head back home to South Carolina by night. I didn't even have the chance to explain matters to my Pastor, Church Family, or friends, but as the day to leave arrived, God spoke to me and said, 'Just Trust Me.' Many things could have gone wrong on the way back, and some came dangerously close. The Lord was with us, and he brought us safely home.

I was being led strongly by the Holy Spirit at this time, and I know for sure that the work the Lord had for me was to start a church right here in my own home community. At first, this ministry was a door-to-door witnessing ministry. My heart was always made glad at how kind so many people were to invite me into their homes and come to our services. The Lord always provided for my family and me. There were some very hard times as well that followed. We all have a cross to bear. We need to remember that there is no easy way to God.

I later met some people who claimed to be friends to me and the people here at the church. However, I slowly but surely came to find out that their main purpose was to take advantage of us. I later found out that they would have gone so far as to destroy our church and our ministry, and they certainly tried everything in their power to do so, but praise be to God, who in his sovereign plan and power, delivered our church from the evil imaginations of those who would have destroyed us, and in a miracle of God, we were completely freed from those people as well and since being given back our freedom, our church could once again begin to flourish. As painful of an experience as this was, especially at the time, I came to understand through it all that what the enemy meant for evil, God meant for good. In a number of ways, God used these hardships to teach me and make me a better minister to the people here.

When I was a boy, I never really knew my father, and that was always hard for me. When some people who were older than me and had so much more experience in the business of the church promised to be there for me and the church and help us, I trusted them and even looked at them as my own father. I put my trust in them, and whatever they told me to do, I tried my best to do it. It was as though I had started to put my trust in them. The Bible tells us that "it is better to trust in the Lord than to put confidence in man" (Psalms 118:8). That verse is in the middle of the Bible, and I am sure that it's there for a reason. I learned the hard way that if there is someone you can put your trust in, who will never fail you, then "trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding, in all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make your paths straight" (Proverbs 3:5-6).

I'm sure that God taught me through all of this to make me a better shepherd to his people and to show me to discern and be on guard against the wrong influence. The Lord has given us so many blessings. We now have a beautiful Church Family which has gotten larger with the birth of my sons, and I have met some truly faithful men of God who have a heart's desire for the work of Christ. One man, in particular, has been so kind to me. I still remember what he told me in trying to bless me by telling me what he has learned in his long ministry. His words to me were, "Remember the vision God gave you and write it all down because the vision is for an appointed time." And now, the vision has come clearly to me in 1993 when the Lord has given me the name for the church: COMMUNITY TEMPLE CHURCH

Our Beliefs

We believe in being baptized in the name of Jesus Christ and receiving the Holy Spirit, with the evidence of speaking in tongues. This is based on the teachings in the Book of Acts, and we hold that this is the biblical plan of salvation.

We believe that Jesus Christ is God manifest in the flesh, fulfilling the role of both the Father and the Son. This understanding is drawn from scriptures such as 1 Timothy 3:16 (KJV), which reveals the mystery of godliness, and Colossians 2:9 (KJV), which confirms that all the fullness of the Godhead dwells in Jesus Christ.

1 Timothy 3:16 (KJV):

“And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, received up into glory.”

Colossians 2:9 (KJV):

“For in him dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily.”

We believe in the necessity of baptism in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins and receiving the Holy Spirit, as demonstrated in the early church through Acts 2:38.

Acts 2:38 (KJV):

“Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost.”

In addition to salvation, we are called to live a life of holiness, set apart for God. Scripture commands that we pursue holiness in all areas of life, because without holiness, no one will see the Lord. Living holy means striving to reflect God's righteousness and obey His commandments. This is rooted in scriptures such as Luke 1:75 and Hebrews 12:14 (KJV), which emphasize living in holiness and righteousness all our days.

Luke 1:75 (KJV):

“In holiness and righteousness before him, all the days of our life.”

Hebrews 12:14 (KJV):

“Follow peace with all men, and holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord.”

We also hold to the belief that God is one, and this oneness is revealed in Jesus Christ. He is both the Father and the Son, as stated in John 10:30 (KJV) and Isaiah 9:6 (KJV), showing that Jesus is the everlasting Father and the Prince of Peace. This doctrine of the oneness of God is central to our faith.

John 10:30 (KJV):

“I and my Father are one.”

Isaiah 9:6 (KJV):

“For unto us a child is born, unto us a son is given: and the government shall be upon his shoulder: and his name shall be called Wonderful, Counsellor, The mighty God, The everlasting Father, The Prince of Peace.”

Furthermore, we believe that speaking in tongues is the initial evidence of receiving the Holy Spirit, as seen in Acts 2:4, Acts 10:44-46, and Acts 19:6. Speaking in tongues is a sign that accompanies the outpouring of the Spirit in a believer’s life.

Acts 2:4 (KJV):

“And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance.”

Acts 10:44-46 (KJV):

“While Peter yet spake these words, the Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word. And they of the circumcision which believed were astonished, as many as came with Peter, because that on the Gentiles also was poured out the gift of the Holy Ghost. For they heard them speak with tongues, and magnify God.”

Acts 19:6 (KJV):

“And when Paul had laid his hands upon them, the Holy Ghost came on them; and they spake with tongues, and prophesied.”

Common Questions

Why should you baptize in Jesus' name?
Baptism in Jesus' name is vital because it follows the example set by the apostles in the New Testament. Acts 2:38 shows that baptism in Jesus' name is directly linked to the remission of sins and the promise of receiving the Holy Spirit. Baptizing in the name of Jesus signifies the believer’s identification with Christ's death, burial, and resurrection (Romans 6:3-4). It's the biblical method of baptism for salvation.
Is speaking in tongues required to show you have received the Holy Spirit?
Yes, speaking in tongues is the initial evidence of receiving the Holy Spirit, according to the scriptures. In Acts 2:4, 10:44-46, and 19:6, believers spoke in tongues when they were filled with the Holy Spirit. While other manifestations of the Spirit may follow, speaking in tongues serves as the outward sign of the infilling of the Holy Ghost in the New Testament church.
Is Jesus both the Father and the Son?
Yes, we believe that Jesus is both the Father and the Son, as revealed in scriptures like John 10:30 and Isaiah 9:6. Jesus is the manifestation of the one true God in the flesh. Colossians 2:9 tells us that the fullness of the Godhead dwells in Jesus bodily, meaning that in Him exists the full presence of the Father and the Son, fulfilling both divine roles in one person.
Why is living a holy life important?
Living a holy life is essential because scripture commands it (Hebrews 12:14). Holiness means being set apart for God and striving to reflect His righteousness in every area of life. Luke 1:75 encourages us to live in holiness and righteousness all our days. Without holiness, no one will see the Lord, which underscores the importance of living according to God’s commands and pursuing a life that reflects His character.
Can Christians listen to secular music?
Whether Christians should listen to secular music is a matter of personal conviction and discernment. Philippians 4:8 encourages believers to focus on things that are true, honorable, and pure. Music has a powerful influence on our minds and spirits, and believers are called to avoid anything that might pull them away from God’s presence. While not all secular music is inherently bad, Christians should evaluate the message, content, and impact it has on their spiritual life. If the music promotes values contrary to holiness, it is best avoided.
Is dancing sinful?
Dancing in itself is not sinful. There are examples in the Bible of people dancing in worship and celebration before the Lord, like King David in 2 Samuel 6:14. However, the context of dancing matters greatly. If the dancing promotes immorality, lust, or behaviors that do not align with a holy life, then it is inappropriate for believers. Christians should ensure that their actions, including dancing, honor God and reflect purity and righteousness.
